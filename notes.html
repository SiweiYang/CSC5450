Tentative schedule

Week 1 (Jan 15): introduction [pdf, one]
  • course overview
  • basic probability: minimum cut, string equality
  • linearity expectation: quicksort, max k-SAT, Ramsey graphs
Week 2 (Jan 22): moments and deviations [pdf, one]
  • balls and bins, coupon collectors
  • Markov inequality, Chebyshev inequality, finding median
  • heuristic argument, Possion approximation
  • power of two choices
Week 3 (Jan 29): Chernoff bounds [pdf, one]
  • Chernoff bounds: proofs and basic examples
  • applications: packet routing, congestion minimization, graph sparsifier
Week 4 (Feb 5): probabilistic method I [pdf, one]
  • graphs: expanders, superconcentrators, high girth high chromatic number
  • entropy: randomness extraction, compression, Shannon's coding theorem
Week 5 (Feb 19): probabilistic method II [pdf, one]
  • second moment method: random graph, threshold behavior
  • local lemma: k-SAT, packet routing, algorithmic proof
Week 6 (Feb 26): random walk [pdf, one]
  • line: 2-SAT, 3-SAT
  • directed graph: stationary distribution, pagerank, perfect matching
  • undirected graph: electrical network, hitting time, cover time
Week 7 (Mar 5): Markov chain Monte Carlo [pdf, one]
  • Monte Carlo: DNF counting, network reliability
  • approximate sampling and approximate counting, Markov chain Monte Carlo
  • coupling: stationary distribution, card shuffling, independent set, random spanning tree
Week 8 (Mar 12): algebraic techniques [pdf, one]
  • polynomial identity testing, Schwarz-Zippel lemma
  • matching, isolation lemma, parallel algorithm
  • network coding
  • matrix rank, independent columns
Week 9 (Mar 19): hashing [pdf, one]
  • hash functions, bloom filters
  • pairwise independence, universal hash functions, perfect hashing
  • applications: finding heavy hitters, derandomization
Week 10 (Mar 26): sublinear algorithms [pdf, one]
  • data streaming: distinct elements, frequency moments
  • random walk: eigenvalues, conductance, local graph partitioning
Week 11 (Apr 2): graph algorithms [pdf, one]
  • color coding
  • minimum spanning trees
  • all pairs shortest paths
  • graph sparsification, cut problems
Week 12 (Apr 9): randomized rounding [pdf, one]
  • LP: set cover, group Steiner tree
  • spectral: Cheeger's inequality
  • SDP: maximum cut, correlation clustering
Week 13 (Apr 16): complexity theory [pdf, one]
  • interactive proofs: graph non-isomorphism, non-satisfiability
  • probabilistically checkable proofs, hardness of approximation
Week 14 (Apr 23): social networks [pdf, one]
  • power law, preferential attachment
  • small world phenomenon, decentralized search
  • epidemics, branching process
